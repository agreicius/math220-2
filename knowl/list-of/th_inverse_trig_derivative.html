Theorem 1.16.6. Derivatives of inverse trig functions.

The following derivative/antiderivative formulas hold:
\begin{align*} \frac{d}{dx} \arcsin x=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}\amp \iff \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}\, dx=\arcsin x+C \amp \text{ (for all \(x\) in \((-1,1)\)) }\\ \frac{d}{dx} \arccos x=-\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}\amp \iff \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}\, dx=-\arccos x+C \amp \text{ (for all \(x\) in \((-1,1)\)) }\\ \frac{d}{dx} \arctan x=\frac{1}{1+x^2}\amp \iff \int \frac{1}{1+x^2}\, dx=\arctan x+C \amp \text{ (for all \(x\)) } \text{.} \end{align*}
