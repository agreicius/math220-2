Example 1.19.4. Odd sine power.

Compute \(\displaystyle \int \sin^3x \cos^2 x\, dx\text{.}\)
Solution.
Peel off a factor of \(\sin x\) and use the substitution \(u=\cos x\text{,}\) \(du=-\sin x\text{:}\)
\begin{align*} \int \sin^3x \cos^2 x\, dx \amp = \int \sin^2x \cos^2 x \sin x\, dx \\ \amp = \int (1-\cos^2x) \cos^2 \sin x\, dx\\ \amp = -\int (1-u^2)u^2\, du \amp (u=\cos x, du=-\sin x dx)\\ \amp = -\frac{1}{3}u^3+\frac{1}{5}u^5+C\\ \amp = -\frac{1}{3}\cos ^3 x+\frac{1}{5}\cos^5 x+C \end{align*}
