Example 1.19.5. Even powers.

Compute \(\displaystyle \int \sin^2 x\cos^4 x\, dx\text{.}\)
Solution.
Both powers are even. We use the square identity to reduce powers across the board:
\begin{align*} \int \sin^2 x\cos^4 x\, dx \amp = \int \frac{1}{2}(1-\cos 2x)\left(\frac{1}{2}(1+\cos 2x)\right)^2\, dx \\ \amp = \frac{1}{8}\int (1-\cos 2x)(1+\cos 2x)^2\, dx\\ \amp = \frac{1}{8}\int 1+\cos 2x-\cos^2 2x-\cos^3 2x\, dx\\ \amp = \frac{1}{8}\int 1-\cos^2 2x+\sin^2 x\cos x\, dx \amp (\cos^3 x=(1-\sin^2 x)\cos x)\\ \amp =\frac{1}{8}\int \frac{1}{2}-\frac{1}{4}\cos 4x+\underset{u^2}{\underbrace{\sin^2 2x}}\, \underset{\frac{du}{2}}{\underbrace{\cos 2x\, dx}} \amp (\cos 2x=\frac{1}{2}(1+\cos 4x))\\ \amp = \frac{1}{16}x-\frac{1}{64}\sin 4x+\frac{1}{48}\sin^3 2x+C \amp (u=\sin 2x, du=2\cos 2x dx)\text{.} \end{align*}
