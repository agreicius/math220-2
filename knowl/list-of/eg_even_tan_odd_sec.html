Example 1.19.8. Even tangent power, odd secant power.

Compute \(\displaystyle \int \sec^3 x\, dx\text{.}\)
Solution.
Not much we can do here besides integration by parts:
\begin{align*} \int \sec^3 x\, dx \amp = \sec x\tan x-\int \sec x\tan^2 x\, dx \amp (u=\sec x, v'=\sec^2 x)\\ \amp = \sec x\tan x-int \sec^3 x-\sec x\, dx \amp (\tan^2 x=\sec^2 x-1) \\ \amp = \sec x\tan x+\ln\abs{\sec x+\tan x}-\int \sec^3 x\, dx\text{.} \end{align*}
Weâ€™ve shown that
\begin{equation*} \int \sec^3 x\, dx=\sec x\tan x+\ln\abs{\sec x+\tan x}-\int \sec^3 x\, dx\text{.} \end{equation*}
Doing some algebra and solving for \(\int \sec^3 x\, dx\text{,}\) we conclude that
\begin{equation*} \int \sec^3 x\, dx=\frac{1}{2}\sec x\tan x+\frac{1}{2}\ln\abs{\sec x+\tan x}+C. \text{.} \end{equation*}
