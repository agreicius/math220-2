Example 1.17.6. Trig identity.

Compute \(\displaystyle \int_0^{\pi/3}\sin^2(2x)\cos(3x) \, dx\text{.}\)
Hint.
Make use of some of the following product-to-sum identities.
\begin{align*} \cos\theta\cos\phi\amp =\frac{\cos(\theta-\phi)+\cos(\theta+\phi)}{2}\\ \sin\theta\sin\phi\amp =\frac{\cos(\theta-\phi)-\cos(\theta+\phi)}{2}\\ \sin\theta\cos\phi\amp = \frac{\sin(\theta-\phi)+\sin(\theta+\phi)}{2}\\ \cos^2\theta\amp =\frac{1+\cos2\theta}{2}\\ \sin^2\theta\amp =\frac{1-\cos2\theta}{2} \end{align*}
Solution.
We have
\begin{align*} \int_0^{\pi/3}\sin^2(2x)\cos(3x) \, dx \amp = \int_0^{\pi/3}\frac{1}{2}(1-\cos(4x))\cos(3x) \, dx \\ \amp = \frac{1}{2}\int_0^{\pi/3}\cos(3x)-\cos(4x)\cos(3x)\, dx\\ \amp =\frac{1}{6}\sin(3x)\vert_0^{\pi/3}-\frac{1}{2}\int\frac{1}{2}(\cos x+\cos(7x))\, dx \\ \amp = 0-\left( \frac{1}{4}\sin x+\frac{1}{28}\sin 7x\right)\Bigr\vert_0^{\pi/3}\\ \amp = -\left( \frac{\sqrt{3}}{8}+\frac{\sqrt{3}}{56}\right)\\ \amp = -\frac{\sqrt{3}}{7}\text{.} \end{align*}
