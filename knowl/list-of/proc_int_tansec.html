Procedure 1.19.3. Integrating \(\tan^m x\sec^n x\).

Let \(m\) and \(n\) be nonnegative integers. When computing
\begin{equation*} \int \tan^m x\sec^n x \, dx \end{equation*}
use the following strategies.
  1. Odd tangent power.
    If \(m=2k+1\) is odd and \(n\) is positive, write
    \begin{equation*} \int \tan^m x\sec^n x \, dx=\int (\sec^2 x-1)^k\sec^{n-1} x\sec x\tan x \, dx \end{equation*}
    and use the substitution \(u=\sec x, du=\sec x\tan x\, dx\text{.}\)
  2. Even secant power.
    If \(n=2k\) is even and positive, write
    \begin{equation*} \int \tan^m x\sec^n x \, dx=\int (\tan^2 x+1)^{k-1}\tan^m x\sec^2 x\, dx \end{equation*}
    and use the substitution \(u=\tan x, du=\sec^2 x\, dx\text{.}\)
  3. Even tangent power, odd secant power.
    If \(m\) is even and \(n\) is odd, express everything in terms of \(\sec x\) and possibly use integration by parts.
  4. Tangent power.
    If \(n=0\text{,}\) use the identity \(\tan^2 x=\sec^2 x-1\) and strategies from the previous cases.
