Solution 1.4.12.1.

In the diagram below we have \(\mathcal{R}=\mathcal{R}_1\cup \mathcal{R}_2\text{.}\) Geometrically we have
\begin{align*} \operatorname{sgn\, area}\mathcal{R} \amp =\operatorname{area}\mathcal{R}_1-\operatorname{area}\mathcal{R}_2\\ \operatorname{area}\mathcal{R} \amp = \operatorname{area}\mathcal{R}_1+\operatorname{area}\mathcal{R}_2 \end{align*}
To compute \(\operatorname{R}\) we use the definition of area:
\begin{align*} \operatorname{area}\mathcal{R} \amp = \int_0^b\abs{f(x)}\, dx\\ \amp =\int_0^1\abs{f(x)}\, dx+\int_1^b\abs{f(x)}\, dx \amp (\knowl{./knowl/th_interval_partition.html}{\text{Interval partition}})\\ \amp \int_0^1 f(x)\, dx -\int_1^b f(x)\, dx \amp (\text{def. of } \abs{f})\\ \amp \frac{3}{4}-((b-1)-(b^4-1) \amp (\knowl{./knowl/eg_int_cubic_cont.html}{\text{1.4.7}})\\ \amp\frac{3}{4}-b+b^4 \text{.} \end{align*}
