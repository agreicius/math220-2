As the diagram below illustrates, each \(x\)-cross section \(S_x\) of the sphere \(\mathcal{S}\) is a circle of radius \(r_x=\sqrt{r^2-x^2}\text{.}\) Thus
\begin{equation*} A(x)=\pi r_x^2=\pi(r^2-x^2) \end{equation*}
and
\begin{align*} \operatorname{vol}\mathcal{S} \amp = \int_{-r}^rA(x)\, dx\\ \amp = \int_{-r}^r\pi (r^2-x^2)\, dx\\ \amp = \pi(r^2x-\frac{1}{3}x^3)\Bigr\vert_{-r}^r\\ \amp = \pi(2r^3-\frac{2}{3}r^3\\ \amp = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3 \end{align*}
as expected!
