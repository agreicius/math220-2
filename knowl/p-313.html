Assume \(f\) is one-to-one and differentiable on the interval \(I\text{,}\) and that \(f'(x)\ne 0\) for all \(x\in I\text{.}\) Let \(J\) be the range of \(f\text{.}\)
  1. The inverse function \(f^{-1}\) is differentiable on \(J\text{.}\)
  2. We have
    \begin{equation} (f^{-1})'(b)=\frac{1}{f'(f^{-1}(b))}\tag{1.10.4} \end{equation}
    for all \(b\in J\text{.}\)
