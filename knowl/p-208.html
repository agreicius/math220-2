Letting \(\displaystyle G(x)=\int_{-2}^x \cos(u^2)\, du\text{,}\) we have
\begin{align*} F(x) \amp = \int_{-2}^{\sin x} \cos(u^2)\, du\\ \amp = G(\sin x) \end{align*}
and thus
\begin{align*} F'(x) \amp =\frac{d}{dx} G(\sin x)\\ \amp = G'(\sin x)\cdot (\sin x)' \amp \text{(chain rule)}\\ \amp = \cos(\sin^2 x)\cdot \cos x \amp (\knowl{./knowl/th_FTC_I.html}{\text{FTC I}}: G'(x)=\cos(x^2)) \end{align*}
