Example 1.3.9.

Let \(n\) be a positive integer and define \(R_n\) to be the right Riemann sum of \(f(x)=1-x^3\) corresponding to the partition of \([0,1]\) into \(n\) equal subintervals.
  1. Derive a closed formula for \(R_n\text{.}\) Your answer will be expressed in terms of \(n\text{.}\)
  2. Compute \(\lim_{n\to \infty}R_n\text{.}\)
  3. Now do the same thing with \(L_n\text{,}\) the left Riemann sum of \(f(x)\) corresponding to the partion of \([0,1]\) into \(n\) equal subintervals.
    Hint. For the closed formula of \(L_n\) use the index shift identity (1.3.2).
