Theorem 1.6.5. FTC I: geometric.

Let \(f\) be continuous on the interval \(I=[a,b]\) and define \(F(x)=\int_a^x f(t)\, dt\text{.}\) In what follows we label the horizontal axis in the plane as the \(t\)-axis.
  1. For all \(x\in I\text{,}\) let \(\mathcal{R}_x\) be the region lying between the graph of \(f\) and the \(t\)-axis, and between the vertical lines \(t=a\) and \(t=x\text{.}\) We have
    \begin{equation} F(x)=\operatorname{sgn\, area} \mathcal{R}_x\tag{1.6.2} \end{equation}
    for all \(x\in I\text{.}\)
  2. At any \(x\in I\) the rate of change of \(\operatorname{sgn \, area} \mathcal{R}_x\) is given by \(F'(x)=f(x)\text{.}\) In other words, the rate of change of the signed area is equal to the (directed) height of the graph of \(f\) at \(x\text{.}\)
