Remark 1.1.12.

It should be noted that the indefinite integral notation is riddled with ambiguity. In particular, whereas the interval of definition \(I\) plays an important role in the definition of antiderivative (as we saw in ExampleÂ 1.1.4), it does not appear in the notation \(\int f\, dx\text{.}\) So how are we to understand a statement like (1.1.5)? We will take it to mean that on some interval (either implied or given) the function \(F\) is an antiderivative of \(f\text{,}\) and hence that the general antiderivative formula for \(f\) on this interval is given by \(G(x)=F(x)+C\text{.}\)
