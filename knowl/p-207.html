Letting \(\displaystyle G(x)=\int_0^x \sin(s^2)\, ds\text{,}\) we have
\begin{align*} F(x) \amp = \int_{4x}^{\sqrt{x^2+1}}\sin(s^2)\, ds\\ \amp = \int_{4x}^{0}\sin(s^2)\, ds+\int_{0}^{\sqrt{x^2+1}}\sin(s^2)\, ds\\ \amp =-\int_{0}^{4x}\sin(s^2)\, ds+\int_{0}^{\sqrt{x^2+1}}\sin(s^2)\, ds\\ \amp =-G(4x)+G(\sqrt{x^2+1})\text{.} \end{align*}
It follows that
\begin{align*} F'(x) \amp = -\frac{d}{dx}G(4x)+\frac{d}{dx}G(\sqrt{x^2+1})\\ \amp = -G'(4x)\cdot 4+G'(\sqrt{x^2+1})(\sqrt{x^2+1})' \amp \text{(chain rule)}\\ \amp = -4G'(4x)+\frac{x\cdot G'(\sqrt{x^2+1})}{\sqrt{x^2+1}}\\ \amp =-4\sin(16x^2)+\frac{x\sin(x^2+1)}{\sqrt{x^2+1}} \amp (\knowl{./knowl/th_FTC_I.html}{\text{FTC I}}: G'(x)=\sin(x^2)) \end{align*}
