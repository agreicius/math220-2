Solution 1.4.11.1.

The region \(\mathcal{R}\) between the graph of \(f\) and the \(x\)-axis, from \(x=1\) to \(x=3\) is a trapezoid of width \(2\text{,}\) and with two heights \(1\) and \(3\text{.}\) (Sketch it for yourself!) Since \(f\geq 0\) the integral of \(f\) computes
\begin{equation*} \operatorname{sgn\, area}\mathcal{R}=\operatorname{area}\mathcal{R}=\frac{1}{2}(1+3)(2)=4\text{.} \end{equation*}
(The last equality uses the area of a trapezoid formula.) This is precisely the value of the integral we computed in ExampleÂ 1.4.3.
