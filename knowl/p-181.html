We have
\begin{align*} \operatorname{sgn\, area}\mathcal{R} \amp = \int_{-\pi}^{\pi}f(x)\, dx\\ \amp =F(\pi)-F(-\pi)\\ \amp =-3(\cos(\pi/3)-\cos(-\pi/3))=0\\ \operatorname{area}\mathcal{R} \amp = \int_a^b\abs{f(x)}\, dx\\ \amp = \int_{-\pi}^0\abs{f}\, dx+\int_0^{\pi}\abs{f} \amp (\knowl{./knowl/th_interval_partition.html}{\text{Interval partition}})\\ \amp = \int_{-\pi}^0-f(x)\, dx+\int_0^\pi f(x)\, dx \amp (\text{def. of } \abs{f})\\ \amp = -\int_{-\pi}^0f(x)\, dx+\int_0^\pi f(x)\, dx\\ \amp =-(F(0)-F(-\pi))+(F(\pi)-F(0))\\ \amp = -(-3\cos 0-(-3)\cos(-\pi/3))+(-3\cos(\pi/3)-(-3)\cos 0)\\ \amp = 6-3=3\text{.} \end{align*}
Below you find a diagram of \(\mathcal{R}\) described as a union \(\mathcal{R}=\mathcal{R}_1\cup \mathcal{R}_2\text{.}\) By the symmetry in the diagram it should come as no surprise that
\begin{equation*} \operatorname{sgn\, area}\mathcal{R}=-\operatorname{area}\mathcal{R}_1+\mathcal{R}_2=0\text{.} \end{equation*}
