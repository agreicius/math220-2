Theorem 1.10.7. Derivative formula for inverses.

Assume \(f\) is one-to-one and differentiable on the interval \(I\text{,}\) and that \(f'(x)\ne 0\) for all \(x\in I\text{.}\) Let \(J\) be the range of \(f\text{.}\) Then:
  1. The inverse function \(f^{-1}\) is differentiable on \(J\text{.}\)
  2. We have
    \begin{equation*} (f^{-1})'(b)=\frac{1}{f'(f^{-1}(b))} \end{equation*}
    for all \(b\in J\text{.}\) Alternatively, letting \(a\) be the unique element of \(D\) such that \(f(a)=b\text{,}\) we have
    \begin{equation*} (f^{-1})'(b)=\frac{1}{f'(a)}\text{.} \end{equation*}
