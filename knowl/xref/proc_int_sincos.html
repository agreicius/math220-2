Procedure 1.19.2. Integrating \(\sin^m x\cos^n x\).

Let \(m\) and \(n\) be nonnegative integers. When computing
\begin{equation*} \int \sin^m x\cos^n x\, dx \end{equation*}
use the following strategies.
  1. Odd sine power.
    If \(m=2k+1\) is odd, write
    \begin{equation*} \int \sin^m x\cos^n x\, dx=\int (1-\cos^2x)^k\cos^n x\sin x\, dx \end{equation*}
    and use the substitution \(u=\cos x, du=-\sin x\, dx\text{.}\)
  2. Odd cosine power.
    If \(n=2k+1\) is odd, write
    \begin{equation*} \int \sin^m x\cos^n x\, dx=\int \sin^m x(1-\sin^2x)^k\cos x \, dx \end{equation*}
    and use the substitution \(u=\sin x, du=\cos x\, dx\text{.}\)
  3. Even powers.
    If \(m\) and \(n\) are both even use \(\displaystyle\sin^2 x=\frac{1-\cos 2x}{2}\) and \(\displaystyle\cos^2 x=\frac{1+\cos 2x}{2}\) to reduce to a lower power of \(\cos 2x\text{.}\)
