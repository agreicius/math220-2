Theorem 1.18.1. Integration by parts.

Let \(u\) and \(v\) be continuously differentiable functions on an interval \(I\) containing the interval \([a,b]\text{.}\)
  1. Indefinite integral form.
    We have
    \begin{equation} \int u(x)v'(x)\, dx= u(x)v(x)-\int u'(x)v(x)\, dx\text{.}\tag{1.18.5} \end{equation}
  2. Definite integral form.
    We have
    \begin{equation} \int_a^b u(x)v'(x)\, dx= u(x)v(x)\Bigr\vert_a^b-\int_a^b u'(x)v(x)\, dx\text{.}\tag{1.18.6} \end{equation}
