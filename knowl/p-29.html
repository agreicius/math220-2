Assume \(F\) is an antiderivative of \(f\) on the interval \(I\text{.}\)
  1. Given any constant \(C\in \R\text{,}\) the function \(G(x)=F(x)+C\) is also an antiderivative of \(f\) on \(I\text{.}\)
  2. Conversely, if \(G\) is an antiderivative of \(f\) on \(I\text{,}\) then there is a constant \(C\in\R\) such that \(G(x)=F(x)+C\) for all \(x\in I\text{.}\)
