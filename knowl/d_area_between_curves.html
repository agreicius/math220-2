Definition 1.8.3. Area of region between curves.

Suppose \(f(x)\geq g(x)\) for all \(x\in [a,b]\text{.}\) Let \(\mathcal{C}_1\) be the graph of \(f\text{,}\) let \(\mathcal{C}_2\) be the graph of \(g\text{,}\) and let \(\mathcal{R}\) be the region between \(\mathcal{C}_1\) and \(\mathcal{C}_2\text{,}\) and between the vertical lines \(x=a\) and \(x=b\text{:}\) i.e.,
\begin{equation*} \mathcal{R}=\{(x,y)\colon a\leq x\leq b, g(x)\leq y\leq f(x)\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
We define the area of \(\mathcal{R}\) to be the integral of \(f-g\) over \([a,b]\text{:}\) i.e.,
\begin{equation*} \text{ area } (\mathcal{R})=\int_a^b f(x)-g(x)\, dx\text{.} \end{equation*}
Similarly, suppose \(x=p(y)\) and \(x=p(q)\) are two functions of \(y\) satisfying \(p(y)\geq q(y)\) for all \(y\in [c,d]\text{.}\) Let \(\mathcal{C}_1\) be the graph of \(p\text{,}\) let \(\mathcal{C}_2\) be the graph of \(q\text{,}\) and let \(\mathcal{R}\) be the region between \(\mathcal{C}_1\) and \(\mathcal{C}_2\text{,}\) and between the horizontal lines \(y=c\) and \(y=d\text{:}\) i.e.,
\begin{equation*} \mathcal{R}=\{(x,y)\colon c\leq y\leq d, p(y)\leq x\leq q(y)\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
We define the area of \(\mathcal{R}\) to be the integral of \(p-q\) over \([c,d]\text{:}\) i.e.,
\begin{equation*} \text{ area } (\mathcal{R})=\int_c^d p(y)-q(y)\, dy\text{.} \end{equation*}
