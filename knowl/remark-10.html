Remark 1.14.7.

Students tend to fall madly in love with l’Hôpital’s rule upon first encountering it. Here are some comments to temper your passion.
  1. Make sure the relevant conditions apply: i.e., (a) \(f\) and \(g\) must be differentiable on an open interval containing \(a\text{,}\) and (b) we must have an indeterminate form of type \(0/0\) or \(\infty/\infty\text{.}\)
  2. Even if l’Hôpital’s rule applies, it may not be useful! In the following examples, applying l’Hôpital’s rule either gets us nowhere, or is too cumbersome.
    \begin{align*} \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{e^x+e^{-x}}{e^{x}-e^{-x}} \amp \amp \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{x^{24}-2x^{22}+2x^2+7}{3x^{24}+x^{20}-x^2+x+1} \text{.} \end{align*}
