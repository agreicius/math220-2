As indicated in the diagram below, the general \(y\)-cross section of this solid will be a washer of outer radius \(R_y=p(y)-0=p(y)\) and inner radius \(r_y=q(y)-0=q(y)\text{.}\) (The \(c\) in (1.9.5) is equal to 0 in this example.) We conclude that
\begin{align*} \operatorname{vol}\mathcal{S} \amp =\int_{-3/2}^2\pi(p(y)^2-q(y)^2)\, dy\\ \amp = \pi\int_{-3/2}^2(4-y^2)^2-(1-y/2)^2\, dy\\ \amp = \frac{2401\pi}{80}\text{,} \end{align*}
after some elementary, if tedious integration.
